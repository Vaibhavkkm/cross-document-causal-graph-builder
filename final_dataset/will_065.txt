Author: Bill
Date: 5 February 1917
Location: London
Nationality: British

Text:


Bhurtpore House  Tidworth  Hants  7th February 1917     My Dear General  I have your   letter to hand advising  me of the C-wealth  Govts. proposal to appoint  McCay as my successor.  & I need hardly say it   has come as a very  great disappointment &  if carried into effect  will do me a great   injustice. I appreciate  indeed your letter and 
reputation of individuals  The raiding all along the line  has been good has it not?  I hope your fellows are keeping  fit & not getting pneumonia  or pleurisy or any chest or  lung trouble. I will come  & see you on your return if  you will let me & shall  hope to find you quite fit.  again. I went to see Joey Davies  had asked about you all he  said was Army Council wouldn't

have it, considering a Lieut  General & KCMG sufficient  for the present of course that   is not especially in view of  the honors scattered about the  war office fathers Smith &  Whighams KCB's it is a  scandal & MS Branch there  feel it is to.  Yours always  Bill.
