France  13/8/1916  Dearest & Best of Mothers  I received  your letter posted 7/8/16 last  evening & am now able to sit  down & write a few lines  in my billet. I have a  nice comfy bed & just imagine  clean sheets & also clean under  clothes. My word! What comfort.  By the time you get this  my little tribe will have arrived  & hope your opinion of them  is favourable.  I am sorry to hear  that Rosie expects another. Poor  little woman, she has enough on  her hands already. Is it  not possible for Ernie to get 
put on home service or  in the government laboratories.  This is no place for him  with that tribe to look after.  Its quite bad enough for me  & If anything should happen to  me Molly & the boys are fairly   well provided for. Not that any  thing is going to happen;  but I  have a feeling that almost amounting  to certain knowledge, I am coming  through alright. So also I believe  has Molly.  I am delighted to hear  you are in communication with  Roy & that he speaks well  of his damaged leg. I was  afraid the poor old chap  would loose his leg.  So glad the Zeppo are  not doing very much damage. 
I think our artillery has them  "bluffed".  Things seem to be going   well on our front. I expect  our battalion will go up to  the firing line again in  about 7 or 8 Days time.  I am in good health  with the exception of a   slight cold & am at this  moment sucking one of your  Brompton Lozenges which you sent  me last winter.  Please give my love  to all at the [["Aust."]] &  keep heaps for yourself  Au-revoir   May God  bless & guard you all.  Ever your loving son  Ernie
